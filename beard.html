Some of the Tips AND Tricks For Growing and Maintaining a BEARD

Tip no. 1

Exfoliate your skin

To grow a beard faster you need to start taking care for your face. Start by cleaning, moisturizing, and exfoliating properly. You should exfoliate your skin once a week, using a scrub or an exfoliant. This will help to remove all the dead skin cells and will stimulate new hair growth.

Tip no. 2

Moisturize your skin

After cleansing your skin, you need to use a moisturizing cream, so that your skin does not feel dry and improve your skin’s appearance by removing dry and flaky skin. Using a moisturizer with eucalyptus can prove to be useful as eucalyptus promotes facial hair growth.

Tip no. 3

Check your face for ingrown hairs

An ingrown hair is nothing but a shaved or tweezed hair that grows back into the skin. This causes an inflammation on your skin. Your beard will not grow in evenly, if there are ingrown hairs on your face. So check if you have ingrown hair. Exfoliating your skin will help you to get rid of the ingrowth.

Tip no. 4

Manage stress

You need to manage stress and rest well so that your damaged skin cells repair themselves. This will promote beard growth. Beards will grow more quickly when you are relaxed. Manage stress by exercising, the improved blood circulation will also promote hair growth.

Tip no. 5

Age

Your age helps determine the rate of your beard’s growth. Males begin to develop facial hair around age 13. Sure, there’s always that one kid in middle school who has a full beard, but typically, the most abundant and fastest hair growth occurs between the ages of 25 and 35. That rate of growth tends to slow down as men age.

Tip no. 6

Do not trim

Do not trim your beard. If you want it to grow long, defy the urge to trim or shape your beard. Groom the beard after about 4 to 6 weeks when it is full, because there is no scientific evidence that suggests that shaving will make your hair grow thicker and quicker.
